IRRI Nomenclature Rules

NOMENCLATURE RULE

EXAMPLE

”IR” cross number designation - for all crosses made in IRRI; assigned by the database administrator of PBGB

IR 53915; IR 58773; IR 88888

 

Segregating generations from IRRI crosses - numbered by generation (SetGen). Numbers are followed by a dash with the first being the F2 plant.

IR 88888 is an F2 plant.

IR 88888-21 is an F3

-          21st selction from IR 88888 F2 pop’n

IR 88888-21-2 is an F4.

-           2nd selection from the IR 88888-21 (F3)

IR 88888-21-2-2 is an F5.

-          2nd selection from the IR 88888-21-2 (F4)

IR 88888 -21-2-2-2 is an F6.

-          2nd selection from the IR 88888-21-2-2 (F5)

If segregating line is not planted in IRRI, the designated name should have a letter representing the site and a space before the plant number;

 

Generations after the location code are assumed to be produced at that location unless another code is encountered.

 

IR 88888-21-2-UBN 2-2 is an F6 plant grown in Ubon, Thailand (UBN) since its F5 generation;

 

IR 88888-21-AJY 2-2-2 is an F6 plant grown in Ajuy, Ilo-ilo (AJY) since its F4 generation.

 

CPA = Chum Phai, Thailand

KKN = Khon Kaen, Thailand

PMI = Phimai, Thailand

SKN = Sakhon Nakhon, Thailand

SRN = Surin, Thailand

UBN = Ubon, Thailand

 

Letter codes that designate breeding methods other than plant selection are as follows:

“B” - bulk,

“R” - rapid generation advance or single seed descent,

“AC” - anther culture.

“C” - for composite populations; a succeeding number indicates the specific cycle of composite

 

 

IR 88888-B is an F3 population that came from BULKED F2 seeds;

IR 88888-R-1 is an F4 from an F3 population in RGA;

IR 88888-AC-1  is the first line selected through anther culture from the cross IR 88888

 

IR 88888-C3-11 is a plant selected from the 3rd  cycle of composite population of IR 88888

Mapping populations will receive an IR cross designation.

Suggested format: IR cross number followed by the plant number and ‘MP’

 IR72746-1MP;

IR 88888-2MP

Fixed lines receive a shortened “IR” designation with the following format:

 

IRYYX###

 

where:

‘YY’ = year the line was bulk-harvested for advanced       evaluation

‘X’ = letter designation for the source breeding program

‘XXX’ is a serial number from the developer; begins at “101”

 

IR05U121 is an upland line (no. 121) bulk-harvested in 2005

 

Breeding Programs + Representative Letters

Irrigated Rice – N, F

Rainfed lowland/aerobic – L

Salinity – T

Upland – U

GUVA – K

Wide Hybridization – W

Hybrid – H

JIRCAS-IRRI Collaboration – J

Anaerobic/ Submergence - D

For hybrid rice, the same rules as those for fixed lines but the letter codes are attached as suffixes of the designation:

H = hybrid,

R = restorer lines,

A = cms line,

B = maintainer line

S = tgms or pgms parent

 

 

IR06H101 is a fixed breeding line developed in the hybrid breeding program

 

IR06H101H is an F1 hybrid

 

IR06H101A is an A line derived from the irrigated breeding program (F)

 

IR06H101S is a TGMS line

The IRRI elite lines will be given a 3- digit number that follows the name “IRRI”, beginning with IRRI 101.

IRRI 101; IRRI 254

IRRI scientists using fixed or elite lines from the IRRI breeding program should use the new names as the primary designation; and can use other popular names as an additional descriptor

 

“IRRI 105 (PsbRc18)”;

“IRRI 132 (Apo)”

Crosses employs a slash (/) to indicate a single cross and

two slashes (//) for the second cross, and so forth.

 

A/B//C

; parent A is crossed to parent B, and the F1 hybrid is crossed to parent C

IR 88888/ IR 53915// IR 58773

Backcrosses

A is the recurrent parent: A*2/B

 B is the recurrent parent: A/2*B

 A/B is the recurrent parent: A/B*2//C

 C is the recurrent parent: A/B//2*C

IR 88888*2/ IR 53915

IR 88888*2/ IR 53915

è IR 88888/IR 88888 // IR 53915

IR 88888/2* IR 53915

è IR 88888//IR 53915 / IR 53915

 

* R in the middle of the Designated Name = RGA or Single Seed Descent

   R in the end of a fixed line name = Restorer line
